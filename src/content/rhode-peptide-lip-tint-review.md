I've been using the [Rhode Peptide Lip Tint](/go/rhode-peptide-lip-tint) for six months now, and I need to talk about whether Hailey Bieber's lip product actually lives up to the Instagram hype. Spoiler: it's more complicated than the glowing reviews suggest.

The Rhode Peptide Lip Tint sits in this weird space between a lip oil and a tinted balm. It promises peptide-powered hydration with a hint of color, but at $16, it's competing with some serious heavy hitters in the lip oil game.

## What Rhode Peptide Lip Tint Actually Does

This isn't your typical lip oil. The formula feels more like a lightweight balm that melts into an oil-like texture on your lips. Rhode claims their peptide complex helps plump and smooth lips over time, while the tint gives you that "your lips but better" color.

The texture is where things get interesting. When you first apply it, there's this almost waxy feel that quickly warms up and becomes glossy. It's not as immediately slippery as traditional lip oils, which honestly works better if you're someone who hates that sliding-around feeling.

I've been rotating between three shades: Ribbon (a berry-pink), Juice Box (peachy-coral), and Toasted Teddy (nude-brown). The color payoff is subtle but buildable. One swipe gives you a hint of tint, two or three layers create more noticeable color.

## The Peptide Promise: Marketing or Magic?

Here's where I get skeptical. Rhode talks up their peptide complex like it's going to transform your lips. After six months of daily use, are my lips noticeably plumper or smoother? Honestly, not really.

Don't get me wrong, my lips feel moisturized and look healthy. But the dramatic before-and-after results you see on social media? I'm not seeing them. The hydration is real, but the peptide benefits feel more like good marketing than game-changing skincare.

What I do notice is that my lips don't get as dry throughout the day when I use this consistently. It's more about maintenance than transformation.

## Rhode Peptide Lip Tint vs. The Competition

### vs. Dior Lip Glow Oil

The [Dior Lip Glow Oil](/go/dior-lip-glow-oil) ($38) is the gold standard everyone compares lip oils to. It's significantly more expensive, but the formula is noticeably more luxurious.

Dior's texture is silkier and feels more premium on the lips. The color selection is better too, with more sophisticated undertones. Where Rhode wins is price and the fact that it doesn't slide off as easily. Dior's formula can feel too slippery for everyday wear.

If you want the most luxurious experience and don't mind spending twice as much, go Dior. If you want something that performs well without the premium price tag, Rhode makes sense.

### vs. Rare Beauty Soft Pinch Tinted Lip Oil

The [Rare Beauty Soft Pinch Tinted Lip Oil](/go/rare-beauty-lip-oil) ($16) is Rhode's closest competitor in price and positioning. Both cost the same, both promise buildable color with hydration.

Rare Beauty's formula is more traditionally oil-like. It's glossier, more slippery, and the color payoff is stronger. If you want more dramatic color and don't mind reapplying throughout the day, Rare Beauty edges ahead.

Rhode's advantage is longevity. It stays put better and doesn't transfer as much onto cups or masks. The trade-off is less dramatic color and shine.

### vs. NYX Fat Oil Lip Drip

The [NYX Fat Oil Lip Drip](/go/nyx-fat-oil) ($8) is the budget option that punches above its weight. At half the price of Rhode, it delivers impressive hydration and a decent color selection.

NYX's formula is thinner and more traditionally oil-like. It's glossier but transfers more easily. The color range is fun and bold, though not as sophisticated as Rhode's curated palette.

For the price, NYX is hard to beat. But Rhode's formula feels more refined and lasts longer on the lips.

## What I Actually Like

**The texture hits a sweet spot.** It's not too waxy, not too slippery. Perfect for someone who wants the benefits of a lip oil without feeling like their lips are sliding around.

**Color selection is thoughtful.** Rhode didn't go crazy with 20 shades. The six available colors are well-curated and flattering on most skin tones.

**It actually stays put.** Unlike traditional lip oils that disappear after your first sip of coffee, this hangs around for a few hours.

**The packaging is solid.** The tube feels substantial, the applicator is the right size, and it doesn't leak in your bag.

## What Drives Me Crazy

**The peptide claims feel overblown.** I'm not seeing the dramatic lip transformation promised in the marketing.

**Limited shade range.** Six colors isn't enough, especially when competitors offer 10-15 options.

**It's not actually that innovative.** Strip away the peptide marketing and celebrity backing, and this is a nice but not revolutionary lip product.

**Price creep.** At $16, it's edging toward premium territory without premium performance.

## The Real-World Test

I wear this most days to work. It survives my morning coffee, looks good in video calls, and doesn't require constant reapplication. That's honestly what I want from a daily lip product.

For special occasions or when I want more drama, I reach for something else. This is your Tuesday morning, running errands, casual dinner with friends product. It's reliable without being exciting.

The staying power is genuinely impressive. I can apply it at 8 AM and still see a hint of color and feel moisturized by lunch. That's better than most lip oils I've tried.

## Who Should Buy Rhode Peptide Lip Tint

You'll love this if you want a low-maintenance lip product that adds subtle color and genuine hydration. It's perfect for people who find traditional lip oils too slippery or high-maintenance.

If you're someone who prefers "your lips but better" over dramatic color, the rhode peptide lip tint delivers exactly that. The formula works especially well if you're constantly drinking coffee or water and need something that won't disappear immediately.

## Who Should Skip It

Pass on this if you want dramatic color payoff or you're looking for a true luxury experience. The [Dior Lip Glow Oil](/go/dior-lip-glow-oil) or [Rare Beauty option](/go/rare-beauty-lip-oil) will serve you better.

Also skip it if you're on a tight budget. The [NYX Fat Oil Lip Drip](/go/nyx-fat-oil) gives you 80% of the experience for half the price.

## Is Rhode Peptide Lip Tint Worth It?

After six months of daily use, I think the Rhode Peptide Lip Tint is a solid but not spectacular product. It does what it promises (subtle color, good hydration, decent longevity) without any major flaws.

The peptide benefits are questionable, and the price feels slightly high for what you get. But if you want a reliable, low-maintenance lip product that looks good and feels comfortable, this delivers.

It's not going to change your life or revolutionize your lip game. But sometimes that's exactly what you need.

## Bottom Line

The Rhode Peptide Lip Tint is a well-executed but unremarkable lip product that costs slightly more than it should. Buy it if you want reliable daily wear and don't mind paying a small premium for celebrity branding. Skip it if you want either luxury performance or budget pricing, both of which you can find elsewhere.
