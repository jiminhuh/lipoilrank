## Dior Lip Glow Oil

This is the lip oil that started the whole trend, and it's still the best for good reason. The formula actually reacts to your lips' pH to create a custom shade that looks like your lips but better.

What sets Dior apart is the texture. It goes on like an oil but transforms into this weightless, glossy finish that doesn't feel sticky or heavy. I can wear this for hours without reapplying, and it actually makes my lips softer over time. The cherry oil base smells incredible without being overwhelming.

The downside? It's expensive at $38, and the color payoff is subtle. If you want bold color, look elsewhere. But for that perfect "your lips but better" effect, nothing beats it.

**Who it's for:** Anyone willing to invest in a luxury lip product that delivers on every promise.

## Rare Beauty Soft Pinch Tinted Lip Oil

Selena Gomez's brand nailed it with this one. The Soft Pinch gives you actual color payoff while still feeling like a nourishing oil. I love how buildable it is, you can go sheer for daytime or layer it up for a more dramatic look.

The formula contains hyaluronic acid and peptides, so it genuinely improves your lip texture over time. I've been using the shade "Joy" (a perfect berry) for months, and my lips are noticeably smoother. It lasts about 4-5 hours with normal eating and drinking.

My only complaint is the applicator. The doe-foot is a bit too big, making precise application tricky. But at $16, it's an incredible value for the quality you get.

**Who it's for:** People who want real color payoff from their lip oil without sacrificing comfort.

[Visit Rare Beauty](/go/rare-beauty-soft-pinch)

## NYX Fat Oil Lip Drip

For $8, this delivers way more than it should. The "Fat Oil" name is accurate, this feels rich and glossy without being goopy. It comes in some really fun shades, including this gorgeous clear with gold flecks that looks amazing over any lipstick.

The wear time isn't as long as pricier options (about 2-3 hours), but the hydration is real. My lips feel soft even after it wears off. The packaging feels a bit cheap, but who cares at this price point?

The main downside is limited shade range compared to other brands. But if you find a color you like, it's an excellent budget pick.

**Who it's for:** Anyone wanting to try the lip oil trend without spending a fortune.

[Try NYX Fat Oil](/go/nyx-fat-oil-lip-drip)

## Clarins Lip Comfort Oil

This was actually the OG lip oil before TikTok made them trendy. Clarins has been perfecting this formula for years, and it shows. The comfort level is unmatched, it feels like you're treating your lips to a spa day.

What I love most is how gentle it is. If you have sensitive or chronically dry lips, this is your answer. The plant oils (including hazelnut and jojoba) actually repair damage instead of just covering it up. The original clear version is my go-to for overnight lip treatment.

The color options are more limited and subtle compared to newer brands. It's also pricey at $32. But for pure comfort and lip health, it's worth every penny.

**Who it's for:** People with sensitive or severely dry lips who need gentle, effective hydration.

[Visit Clarins](/go/clarins-lip-comfort-oil)

## e.l.f. Glow Reviver Lip Oil

At $10, this is e.l.f.'s attempt at the viral lip oil trend. It does the job, but just barely. The formula is thinner than others on this list, more like a glossy serum than a true oil. It gives a nice shine and light hydration, but don't expect miracles.

The color range is decent for the price, and I actually like the minty tingle you get when you first apply it. It's refreshing. But the wear time is short (maybe 2 hours), and it doesn't provide the long-term lip benefits of pricier options.

Honestly, I'd rather save up for the NYX or spend a bit more for Rare Beauty. But if $10 is your budget, it's not terrible.

**Who it's for:** Beginners who want to try lip oil without any investment or commitment.

[Try e.l.f. Glow Reviver](/go/elf-glow-reviver)

## Rhode Peptide Lip Tint

Hailey Bieber's brand launched with a lot of hype, and this lip oil is... fine. It's very much in line with the Rhode aesthetic: minimal, clean, expensive-feeling. The packaging is gorgeous, and the formula feels luxurious.

The peptides are supposed to plump your lips over time, and I did notice a subtle difference after a few weeks of use. The tint is very natural-looking, almost like you just bit your lips. It's perfect if you're going for that no-makeup makeup look.

But for $16, I expected more. The hydration is good but not exceptional, and the color payoff is so subtle it's almost pointless. It's more about the vibe than the performance.

**Who it's for:** Minimalists who prioritize aesthetics and subtle enhancement over dramatic results.

## How We Picked These

I tested each lip oil for at least two weeks, wearing them in different situations: alone, over lipstick, during meals, and overnight. I evaluated them on hydration (both immediate and long-term), color payoff, wear time, comfort, and value for money.

I also considered ingredients, focusing on oils that actually nourish lips rather than just sitting on the surface. The best lip oils should improve your lip texture over time, not just provide temporary shine.

Finally, I factored in real-world usability. The prettiest formula in the world doesn't matter if the applicator is terrible or if it disappears after one sip of coffee. These recommendations reflect products that actually work in daily life, not just in perfect testing conditions.
